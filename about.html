About Me

Who I Am

Hello! I'm Rohini Ramakrishna, a Senior Software Developer with over 10 years of experience in creating robust, scalable solutions across various domains. Based in Munich, Germany, I specialize in Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, JavaScript, and cloud-based technologies.

My journey in software development began in 2008, and since then, I've worked on a diverse range of projects including B2B platforms, cloud infrastructure, loan management systems, Payroll and time management applications. I'm passionate about building clean, maintainable code and creating solutions that solve real business problems.

What drives me is the opportunity to transform complex requirements into elegant, user-friendly applications. I enjoy the challenge of optimizing performance, enhancing scalability, and ensuring reliability in the systems I develop.

My Approach

I believe in a thoughtful, collaborative approach to software development. Every project begins with a deep understanding of the requirements and business goals. I value clean code, test-driven development, and continuous integration to ensure quality and maintainability.

Throughout my career, I've found that the most successful projects are those where communication is open and transparent. I enjoy working closely with product managers, designers, and fellow developers to create solutions that truly meet user needs.

I firmly adhere to fundamental software principles like KISS (Keep It Simple, Stupid), DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself), and SOLID. My code philosophy emphasizes readability—variable names, method names, and class names should make code as easy to understand as English. While I believe comments are essential for complex logic, well-written code should largely speak for itself.

Unit testing is central to my workflow, as it boosts confidence in the code and simplifies debugging during edge cases. I also understand the importance of documentation and make it a priority to document during the development process itself rather than treating it as an afterthought.
